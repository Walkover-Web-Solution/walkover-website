# Walkover Website — AI System Rules (MANDATORY, V2 — PRODUCTION)

Every change to user-facing copy, hero, product pages, careers, CTAs, SEO, or tone MUST follow the rules below. They override generic design instinct. Do not ship a line that violates any rule here.

---

# WALKOVER WEBSITE AI SYSTEM (V2 — PRODUCTION)

## 0. CORE TRUTH (DO NOT BREAK)

We are not selling jobs.
We are attracting builders.

Everything on the website must answer:
👉 "What will I get to build here?"

---

## 1. POSITIONING (LOCKED)

Walkover = Startup Infrastructure Layer

We build systems so startups don't rebuild the same things.

Products include:

* Communication (MSG91)
* Automation (viaSocket)
* Accounting backbone (Giddh)
* AI systems (emerging)

DO NOT describe us as:

* IT company
* Software services company
* Generic startup

---

## 2. NARRATIVE SYSTEM (CRITICAL)

### Rule:

Walkover = Builder of systems
Products = Where real work happens

### Never mix randomly.

#### Homepage:

* Show **what we build (products)**
* Not deep product features
* Focus: "this is the kind of work you'll do"

#### Product pages:

* Fully independent
* No heavy Walkover branding

#### Careers page:

* Deep culture + working style
* Real expectations

---

## 3. HOMEPAGE STRUCTURE (MANDATORY)

Order:

1. **Belief (hook)**
2. **What we build (products as proof)**
3. **Why this matters (for builders)**
4. **How we work (culture, real not fluffy)**
5. **CTA (Join Us)**

---

## 4. HERO SYSTEM

Format:

[Contrarian or sharp belief]
[What we do]
[Who it's for]

Example:

Stop rebuilding the same things.
We build infrastructure for startups
so builders can focus on what actually matters.

---

## 5. PRODUCT SHOWCASE RULE

Each product must answer:

* What problem it removes
* Why it matters
* One proof (scale / usage)

Example:

MSG91
Handles communication at scale.
1B+ messages every month.

---

## 6. PROOF SYSTEM (MANDATORY)

Every page must include at least one:

* Scale (e.g., 1B SMS)
* Adoption
* Real usage
* Longevity

Avoid empty claims.

---

## 7. CULTURE SYSTEM (NO FLUFF)

DO NOT say:

* "Happy"
* "Flexible"
* "Work-life balance"

SHOW instead:

* How decisions are made
* Ownership level
* Speed of execution
* Real expectations

Example:

Bad:
"We believe in flexibility"

Good:
"No approvals for small decisions.
Ship fast. Fix later."

---

## 8. TONE SYSTEM

Base tone:

* Minimal
* Clear
* Slightly philosophical

Allowed:

* Light wit
* Occasional bold lines

NOT allowed:

* Over-smart sarcasm
* Meme tone
* Corporate tone

---

## 9. WRITING RULES

* Short lines
* No paragraph > 3 lines
* Use rhythm

Example:

"We build fast.
We break things.
We fix them faster."

---

## 10. SEO SYSTEM (HIRING-FIRST)

Primary intent:
👉 Talent acquisition (India)

### Page-level rules:

Homepage:

* "startup to work for in India"
* "product startup India"

Careers:

* "startup jobs India"
* "software engineer startup India"
* "AI startup jobs India"

Blog:

* builder stories
* engineering insights
* product thinking

---

## 11. CTA SYSTEM

Primary CTA:
👉 Join Us

Secondary:

* Explore Products

Rules:

* No long CTA text
* No emotional manipulation

---

## 12. ANTI-GENERIC FIREWALL

Before publishing ANY line:

Ask:

1. Can this be said by any startup?
   → If yes, delete
2. Is this showing real work?
   → If no, rewrite

---

## 13. COMPONENT SYSTEM (REUSABLE)

### A. Builder Hook Block

"What you'll work on here"

### B. Product Proof Block

Name + problem + scale

### C. Culture Reality Block

"How we actually work"

### D. Philosophy Line

Short, deep, optional

---

## 14. PRODUCT VS PARENT RULE

DO NOT:

* Over-explain Walkover on product pages

DO:

* Let products stand alone

Homepage:

* Connect them as ecosystem

---

## 15. FINAL FILTER

Every page must feel like:
👉 "This is a place where serious builders work on real systems"

If it feels like:

* HR page ❌
* College brochure ❌
* Generic startup ❌

Rewrite.

---

## 16. AI SELF-CHECK LOOP

Before output:

* Is this sharp?
* Is this real?
* Is this builder-first?

If not → rewrite automatically.

---

## 17. WHEN TO BREAK THESE RULES

These rules are default.

They can be broken when:

* Writing deeply technical content
* Explaining product features in detail
* SEO requires longer structured content

But even then:

* Stay clear
* Avoid generic tone

---

## 18. PRODUCT NAMING RULE

Always use exact product names:

* MSG91 (not Msg91, not msg91)
* viaSocket (keep casing consistent)
* Giddh

Never rename or simplify product names.

---

## 19. PHILOSOPHY LIMIT

Philosophical lines are accents, not content.

Rules:

* Max 1–2 per page
* Must feel natural
* Never replace clarity

If it sounds like a quote page → remove it
